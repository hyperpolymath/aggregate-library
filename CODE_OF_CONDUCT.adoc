== Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the aggregate-library community a harassment-free, emotionally safe, and welcoming experience for everyone, regardless of:

* Age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics
* Gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status
* Nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation
* Technical skill level, programming language preference, or paradigm affiliation

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community that prioritizes *emotional safety* and *psychological well-being*.

== Our Standards

=== Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

* *Kindness and Empathy*: Treating others with compassion and understanding
* *Respectful Disagreement*: Engaging constructively with differing opinions and experiences
* *Constructive Feedback*: Providing helpful, specific, and actionable feedback
* *Accountability*: Accepting responsibility and apologizing when mistakes are made
* *Community Focus*: Prioritizing what is best for the overall community
* *Emotional Safety*: Recognizing and respecting emotional boundaries
* *Patience*: Understanding that people have different backgrounds and skill levels
* *Celebration*: Acknowledging contributions and achievements of others
* *Learning Mindset*: Approaching interactions with curiosity and openness

=== Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

* *Harassment*: Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks
* *Discrimination*: Discriminatory jokes, language, or imagery
* *Invasion of Privacy*: Publishing others' private information without permission
* *Unwelcome Conduct*: Sexual attention or advances of any kind
* *Intimidation*: Threats of violence or violent language
* *Disruption*: Sustained disruption of discussions or collaboration
* *Gatekeeping*: Discouraging participation based on perceived skill level
* *Dismissiveness*: Invalidating others' experiences or concerns
* *Toxicity*: Creating or maintaining a hostile, uncomfortable, or unsafe environment
* *Bad Faith*: Deliberately misrepresenting others' positions or intentions

== Emotional Safety

=== Our Commitment

This project explicitly values *emotional safety* as a core principle:

* *Low-Stress Environment*: We strive to minimize anxiety and stress in interactions
* *Reversibility*: Mistakes are learning opportunities; contributions can be reverted
* *Psychological Safety*: People should feel safe to experiment, ask questions, and fail
* *No Shaming*: We never shame people for not knowing something or making mistakes
* *Consent-Aware*: We respect boundaries and allow people to opt out
* *Well-Being Priority*: Community member well-being takes precedence over project velocity

=== Reducing Anxiety

To maintain low anxiety levels:

* *Clear Documentation*: Comprehensive guides reduce uncertainty
* *Transparent Processes*: Predictable contribution workflows
* *Supportive Feedback*: Focus on improvement, not criticism
* *No Urgency Pressure*: Contributions happen at comfortable paces
* *Safe Experimentation*: Sandbox branches and testing environments
* *Respect for "No"*: Accepting when people decline tasks or need breaks

== Enforcement Responsibilities

=== Maintainers

Community leaders and maintainers are responsible for:

* Clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior
* Taking appropriate and fair corrective action
* Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, issues, and contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
* Communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate
* Prioritizing the emotional safety and well-being of community members

=== Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces and when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples include:

* Project repositories (code, issues, PRs, discussions)
* Official communication channels (email, chat, forums)
* Social media accounts representing the project
* Conferences, meetups, and events
* Any other forums where community members interact

== Enforcement

=== Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

* *Email*: conduct@[project-domain] (when available)
* *Private Issue*: Contact maintainers listed in link:MAINTAINERS.md[]

*All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.*

=== Confidentiality

All community leaders are obligated to:

* Respect the privacy and security of the reporter
* Keep details confidential unless necessary for investigation
* Protect reporters from retaliation

=== Investigation Process

. *Acknowledgment*: Reporter receives confirmation within 72 hours
. *Initial Review*: Maintainers assess the report (1 week)
. *Investigation*: Gather context and perspectives as needed
. *Decision*: Determine appropriate response
. *Communication*: Inform involved parties of outcome
. *Appeal*: Allow for appeal process if requested

== Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines when determining consequences:

=== 1. Correction

*Community Impact*: Minor, isolated incident of inappropriate behavior

*Consequence*:

* Private written warning
* Explanation of violation
* Request for apology or behavior change

=== 2. Warning

*Community Impact*: Single significant violation or series of minor violations

*Consequence*:

* Warning with consequences for continued behavior
* Temporary suspension from specific spaces or interactions
* Required acknowledgment of behavior change

*Duration*: Specified time period or until conditions met

=== 3. Temporary Ban

*Community Impact*: Serious violation or sustained inappropriate behavior

*Consequence*:

* Temporary ban from all community interaction
* No public or private interaction with community members
* May include ban from pull requests, issues, and discussions

*Duration*: Specified time period (typically 30-90 days)

=== 4. Permanent Ban

*Community Impact*: Pattern of violations, severe violation, or harassment

*Consequence*:

* Permanent ban from all community spaces
* Prohibited from all public interaction
* Removal of contribution access

== Appeals

Individuals who receive enforcement actions may appeal by:

. Emailing maintainers within 14 days of decision
. Providing context or new information
. Requesting reconsideration of penalty

Appeals will be reviewed by maintainers not involved in original decision (when possible).

== Restorative Justice

Where appropriate, we favor *restorative justice* approaches:

* *Understanding*: Helping violators understand impact of actions
* *Repair*: Opportunities to make amends and rebuild trust
* *Learning*: Growth and behavior change over punishment
* *Community Healing*: Repairing harm done to community
* *Reintegration*: Pathways back to good standing (when safe)

Restorative justice is *not* appropriate for:

* Severe harassment or threats
* Repeated violations after multiple chances
* Cases where victim/community requests otherwise
